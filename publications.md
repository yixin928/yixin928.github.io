## Peer-reviewed Journal Papers
- **Throwing the baby out with the bathwater: discursive contradictions in the feminist critiques of pro-feminist men**<br>Hao Cao, **Yixin Huang**†, Kai Guo, Yuwen Peng, Can Wang<br>[*Feminist Media Studies*]([tandfonline.com/rfms20](https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/rfms20))<br>doi: [10.1080/14680777.2026.2620773](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/14680777.2026.2620773)<br>**Abstract**<br>Recent studies indicate that pro-feminist men have increasingly engaged in gender justice activism, especially through hashtag activism. Yet their feminist interventions have led to both progresses and liabilities in enhancing women’s rights. Such contradictionary ramifications became heightened in China where platform-backed misogyny made visible both their pro-feminist endeavors and misconducts. Against recent sexual misconducts by alleged male feminists, this study sets to explore how Chinese feminist critics responded to the paradoxical effects of men’s pro-feminist engagements. Using qualitative interviews and textual analysis, the findings show that feminist critics’ informed critique of pro-feminist men paradoxically led to increasingly exclusivist understanding of feminism when similar or stricter standards of feminist performances were applied to female and other gender non-conforming feminists. We locate such discursive contradictions in platform-backed misogyny that simultaneously directed attention to the patriarchal underpinnings of pro-feminist men’s privileges and deflected attention from the power asymmetries between feminists with different gender identities. This study, therefore, contributes to extending digital gender studies by identifying and accounting for the new discursive contradictions in men’s pro-feminist engagements.<br>

- **The Manufacture of Soft Eroticism in Complicit Edge Ball Games: Platformized Bodies and Relational Moderation on Chinese Short-video/Live-streaming Platforms**<br>Under minor revision at [*Television and New Media*](https://journals.sagepub.com/home/tvn)<br>**Abstract**<br>Eroticism has surged across social feeds as a lucrative trend. In China’s hyper‑monitored digital landscape, however, it not only survives but thrives in unexpected, diverse forms. To explore this alternative platform-based cultural creation, this study examines the risky yet prevalent soft eroticism known as cabian (擦边 content and practices that hit the edge of rules). Drawing on online and offline participatory observation, we investigate how soft eroticism is manufactured through the interplay of content production and moderation. The findings demonstrate that cabian reconfigures “platformized bodies” by weaving semiotic, technical, and moral bodies into subjective eroticism situated in a regulatory liminal zone. Accordingly, content moderation has evolved from relevant content filtering into “relational moderation,” targeting the networks among users, platforms, and the state that sustain monetized eroticism production. The co-construction of content production and moderation reveals that embodiment not only embeds within but actively reshapes the logic of platformization.<br>
